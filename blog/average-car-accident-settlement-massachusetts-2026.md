## Why Massachusetts Settles Higher Than Most States

### 1. Generous Pain & Suffering Multipliers

Our data shows Massachusetts averages **3.7x medical specials** for pain and suffering, versus a national average of 2.8x. Adjusters know Massachusetts juries are plaintiff-friendly, so they settle higher to avoid trial.

### 2. Chapter 93A Bad Faith Threat

M.G.L. Chapter 93A allows **double or triple damages** plus attorney fees for insurer bad faith. Simply invoking 93A in a demand letter often unlocks settlement movement. This is Massachusetts's most powerful negotiation tool.

### 3. Modified Comparative Negligence (51% Bar)

M.G.L. c. 231, § 85 bars recovery at 51% fault but allows recovery at 50%. More plaintiff-friendly than contributory negligence states where 1% fault = $0.

| Client Fault | Recovery Impact |
|-------------|----------------|
| 0-25% | Full negotiating leverage |
| 26-50% | Viable, discounted proportionally |
| 51%+ | No recovery |

### 4. Collateral Source Rule

Massachusetts doesn't let defendants reduce settlements because health insurance paid the bills. Your client's $50,000 hospital bill counts at full value even if insurance covered it. In other states, it might count as $20,000.

### 5. Low PIP Threshold

Massachusetts is a no-fault state, but the threshold is only **$2,000 in reasonable medical expenses**. Most injury cases clear this easily, opening the door to full tort claims.

## The 4 Factors That Drive Massachusetts Case Value

### Factor 1: Medical Specials

The foundation of every demand. Massachusetts multipliers from our data:

| Injury Status | Multiplier Range |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Full recovery expected | 1.5-3x specials |
| Permanent partial disability | 3-5x specials |
| Permanent total disability | 5-10x+ specials |

**Example:** $40,000 specials with permanent partial impairment → demand range of $120,000-$200,000.

### Factor 2: Permanency

Permanent impairment ratings are the single biggest value driver. A doctor's statement of "permanent partial impairment" can double the multiplier. Always push for a thorough AMA Guidelines impairment evaluation at MMI.

### Factor 3: Liability Documentation

Police reports assigning fault are worth $20,000-$50,000 in settlement leverage. Clear liability cases (rear-end, red light) settle at 90-100% of calculated value. Disputed cases settle at 50-70%.

### Factor 4: Policy Limits

Massachusetts minimums: $20,000/$40,000. Average policies: $100,000/$300,000. **Always investigate UIM coverage at intake.** A $200,000 injury against minimum-coverage defendants nets $20,000 without UIM.
