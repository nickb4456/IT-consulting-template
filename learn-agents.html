What Are AI Agents?
Explained Simply

AI Agents are like having a brilliant assistant who can actually do things for you — not just answer questions. Learn how they work in plain English.

Start Learning Get Full Presentation
Drag to rotate the neural network
1

Think of an AI Agent as Your Digital Employee

Traditional AI (like basic ChatGPT) is like a really smart consultant. You ask questions, it answers. But an AI Agent? That's like hiring someone who can actually do the work.

Regular AI

You: "How do I organize these files?"
AI: "Here are 5 steps to organize files..."

It tells you what to do. You still do the work.

AI Agent

You: "Organize my files by date"
Agent: *Actually organizes them*

It does the work for you. You approve the results.

The Key Difference

AI Agents can take actions: browse the web, write files, run code, send emails, interact with apps.

They're AI with hands, not just a brain.

2

Every AI Agent follows this simple pattern. Click each step to see it in action.

Step 1: You Describe What You Want

Use plain English. Don't worry about technical terms — just be specific about the outcome you want.

you → "Create a report of all my customers who haven't ordered in 90 days, with their last order date and total spent"

What Just Happened

  • You described what you wanted in plain English
  • The agent figured out how to do it
  • It wrote actual working code
  • It configured your computer to run it automatically
  • It asked before doing anything permanent

You're Always In Control

  • The agent shows you what it plans to do first
  • You approve before any action is taken
  • You can say "wait, change this part"
  • Nothing happens without your permission
  • You can undo anything it does
