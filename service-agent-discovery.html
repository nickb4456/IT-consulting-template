Free Discovery Call

You know AI could help your business. You've seen the demos, read the articles. But where do you actually start? Which tasks are worth automating? What's realistic vs. hype?

That's what the discovery call is for. We sit down (or hop on a video call), and I ask you one simple question: What takes up too much of your time?

From there, we map out your workflows — the emails you answer over and over, the data you copy-paste between systems, the scheduling back-and-forth, the documents that need reviewing. We find the patterns.

What You'll Get

After our call, I'll send you a short report (not a 40-page sales deck) that includes:

  • The Top 3 Automation Opportunities — Tasks that are eating your time and are actually good candidates for AI
  • Estimated Time Savings — Hours per week you'd get back, based on what you told me
  • Rough Cost Estimate — What it would take to build and maintain each automation
  • The "Don't Bother" List — Things that sound automatable but aren't worth it (yet)
"I went in thinking I needed a chatbot. Turns out my real problem was invoice processing — I was losing 6 hours a week to it. Never would have figured that out on my own."

How It Works

Step 1: The Call (30-45 min)

We talk through your day-to-day. I'll ask about the tasks you dread, the ones that feel repetitive, the bottlenecks. No preparation needed — just show up and be honest about where things are messy.

Step 2: The Analysis (1-2 days)

I take what we discussed and figure out what's actually automatable with current AI. Some things are easy wins. Others sound good but aren't ready for prime time. I'll be straight with you about which is which.

Step 3: The Report

You get a one-page summary with my recommendations, time estimates, and ballpark costs. If you want to move forward, great — we'll scope a project. If not, you still walk away knowing exactly what's possible.

Why Free?

Because most businesses don't know what they don't know. I'd rather have a real conversation about your problems than pitch you a solution you might not need.

And honestly? About half the people I talk to aren't ready for AI automation yet — and I'll tell them that. No point building something that won't get used.
