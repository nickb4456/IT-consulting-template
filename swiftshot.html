Case Study

The screenshot that cost a law firm $340,000.

Federal Court. Cross-Examination.

"Your Honor, opposing counsel has presented a screenshot as evidence. But they cannot prove it hasn't been altered. I move to exclude."

Motion granted.

The paralegal had taken the screenshot three months earlier. A defamatory social media post. Clear evidence. She'd even used the firm's standard naming convention. Saved it in the case folder.

But when opposing counsel asked one simple question — "Can you prove this image hasn't been modified since capture?" — there was no answer.

No hash verification. No timestamp from a neutral source. No chain of custody. Just a PNG file that could have been edited in Photoshop five minutes before the hearing.

The evidence was excluded. The case settled for $340,000 less than it should have. Three months of discovery work, gone in 30 seconds.

The managing partner called a meeting that afternoon.

"This can never happen again."
