Case File: Evidence Dismissed
Federal Court, Boston. 9:47 AM.

"Counsel, can you prove this screenshot hasn't been altered since it was taken?"

— Opposing counsel, during cross-examination

The paralegal had taken the screenshot three months earlier. A social media post. Clear evidence of defamation. But she couldn't prove it was authentic.

No hash verification. No timestamp from a trusted source. No chain of custody. Just a PNG file that could have been edited in Photoshop five minutes before the hearing.

The judge excluded it. Three months of discovery work, thrown out in 30 seconds.

The partner called a meeting that afternoon. "This can never happen again."
